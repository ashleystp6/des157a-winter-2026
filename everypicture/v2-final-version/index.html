My Lego-tastic Creations!

Succulents

I built this succulent set when I first transferred to UC Davis last year. I placed them in various places around my old apartment. In my current apartment they are placed above the sink in the kitchen. These succulents include Britton's Liveforever, Eastern Prickly Pear, Pincushion Cactus, Venus Flytrap, Red Sundew, Yellow Pitcher Plant, False Shamrock, Jade Plant, and Laceleaf.

Cat

I was gifted this cat set from my family for Christmas this past year and it somewhat resembles the coloring of my cat I had as a child. It's perched on top of this wall in my kitchen so it can overlook the living room. This set is very customizable for the user because it came with different colored eyes and an open or closed mouth that can be switched out. The ears, front paws, and end of the tail can also be moved to show expression in the cat.

Love

This LOVE set came out around Valentine's Day and now I have it displayed in my bedroom. This set was orignially a painting by Robert Indiana in 1965. This painting was used by the Museum of Modern Art in New York for their annual holdiay card. In 1970, his first monumental LOVE sculpture was created and is now located in the Indianapolis Museum of Art.

Orchid

This orchid set was one of the first legos from their plant collection that I built. Orchids are one of my favorite flowers because of their varity of colors, sizes, and overall beauty. This set is currently on my desk at my apartment so I can look at it all the time.

Flower Bouquet

This vibrant bouquet of many species of flowers was very enjoyable to build. There are about 9 different species in the set with a variety of colors and fun shapes which makes for an interesting bouquet decoration. Some of these plants are Cornflowers, Eucalyptus, Lily of the Valley, and more.
